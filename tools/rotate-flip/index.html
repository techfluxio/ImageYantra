Guide

How to Rotate & Flip Images — Fix Orientation Issues

Sideways photos are frustrating — and surprisingly common. Smartphones embed orientation data in EXIF metadata, but many platforms ignore it, displaying the image rotated 90°. This tool bakes the correction directly into the pixels so your image displays correctly everywhere.

Rotation vs Flip

Rotate 90° / 180° / 270° — turns the entire image clockwise or counter-clockwise. Use to fix a sideways or upside-down photo. Flip Horizontal — creates a mirror image (left-right reversal). Useful for selfie mirror correction or design symmetry. Flip Vertical — top-to-bottom reflection, used for artistic effects.

Common Use Cases

  • Fix phone photos that appear sideways when uploaded to websites or forms
  • Correct scanned documents from flatbed scanners that don't auto-orient
  • Mirror product images for consistent left-facing catalog display
  • Create reflection effects for product or landscape photography
  • Fix upside-down photos from cameras without auto-orientation

Why Not Use Your Phone Gallery?

Phone galleries usually only update EXIF orientation metadata — not the actual pixel data. The image looks correct on your phone but displays sideways when uploaded to other platforms. ImageYantra performs true pixel-level rotation, ensuring compatibility everywhere.
