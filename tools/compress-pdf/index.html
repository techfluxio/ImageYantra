Guide

How to Compress PDF Files — Why It Matters

PDFs are a container format — they hold text, fonts, and embedded images. The images are almost always the largest component, and re-compressing them at a lower quality dramatically reduces the file size without affecting text readability at all.

When You Need to Compress

  • Government and university portals with 1–2 MB upload limits
  • Email attachments (most providers limit to 10–25 MB)
  • WhatsApp document sharing (100 MB limit, but smaller loads faster)
  • Scanned document archives to save storage space
  • Job applications — resumes and portfolios sent by email

100% Private Processing

Your PDF never leaves your device. All compression is performed client-side in your browser using pdf-lib.js. No server upload, no data retention, no watermarks added — ever.
